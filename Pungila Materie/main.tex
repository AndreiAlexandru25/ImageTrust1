\section*{Abstract}
Most AI-generated image detectors perform poorly under social-media recompression and do not output calibrated uncertainty estimates. This thesis proposes ImageTrust, a forensic system that addresses both shortcomings. ImageTrust concatenates embeddings from ResNet-50 (2,048-d), EfficientNet-B0 (1,280-d), and ViT-B/16 (768-d) into a single 4,096-dimensional representation, classified by both XGBoost and MLP meta-classifiers. We employ temperature scaling to reduce calibration error (ECE\,=\,0.016 for XGBoost) and conformal prediction to provide 95.2\% coverage guarantees with explicit abstention. Training on 604,589 images across four compression variants (original, WhatsApp, Instagram, screenshot) results in less than 0.3\% AUC degradation under social-media recompression, compared to 3--8\% in prior detectors. The system is deployed as a PySide6 desktop application and Next.js/FastAPI web application with offline support.

\noindent\textbf{Keywords}: AI-generated image detection, backbone fusion, probability calibration, conformal prediction, uncertainty quantification.
